Innovation Project — Manhattan Project legacy contamination

Old Manhattan Project sites — like the St. Louis FUSRAP sites and Hanford — left behind radioactive contamination. People living nearby ask: could this be making us sick, and how does it spread? We built two interactive tools to explore those questions honestly, using simulated data and clearly-labeled real-world sources.

Cancer–Radiation Exposure Correlation Explorer

A map and statistics tool that explores whether living closer to a contamination site lines up with higher cancer rates — and teaches why correlation is not causation.

💧

Groundwater Contamination Plume Simulator

A simplified model of how radioactive waste travels through groundwater — adjust soil, flow, depth, and radioactive decay to see how far a plume could spread and which towns it reaches.

Open tool →
Honesty note: Both tools are educational student prototypes. They use simulated data and simplified models, not real patient records or validated scientific simulations. Real studies would require professional tools, expert scientists, IRB approval, and field data.
